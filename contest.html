Challenge

The primary focus of the CLASS initiative is to address the gap in perception and understanding regarding climate change among stakeholders in rural communities, and to enhance the educational curriculum in high schools and higher education institutes with a focus on climate change. The Climate Change Curriculum Challenge is a competitive opportunity for educators to work together to compile a curated collection of artifacts that any teacher can pick up and use to catalyze their students in climate change science.

Objective

Given the participants in the CLASS conference, groups will work to develop a curriculum piece that includes lesson plans for 1 week of content, a lab activity, and an assessment tool based on local interest for climate change science that will be submitted 1 week after the conference. Given the participants in the CLASS conference, groups will work to develop a 2-minute elevator style pitch that will be presented during the final session on the conference based on their proposal for their climate change science curriculum piece.
