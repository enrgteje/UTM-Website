Anna Gafford

Undergrate Researcher

Anna Gafford is an undergraduate student of Agricultural Economics in the Department of Agriculture, Geosciences, and Natural Resources at the University of Tennessee at Martin. She is a junior agricultural business major with a minor in business administration. Ms. Gafford is a member of The National Society of Leadership and Success. She is currently working on a USDA research grant for Building Capacity for Climate Change Education (CCE) and Expanding Research Opportunities in Rural Communities in Tennessee and Kentucky. Ms. Gafford is currently conducting a research study on calculating carbon emissions from agricultural production in West Tennessee, specifically through no-till and conventional till cropping practices. She has recently presented her research findings for An Initial Study on Calculating Carbon Emissions from Agricultural Production in West Tennessee at the 5th Annual International Conference on Climate Change 2021.
