People

Rachna Tewari, Ph.D.

Principal Investigator
Associate Professor of Agricultural Economics
College of Agricultural and Applied Sciences
265 Brehm Hall
rtewari@utm.edu

Dr. Rachna Tewari is Associate Professor of Agricultural Economics in the Department of Agriculture, Geosciences, and Natural Resources at the University of Tennessee at Martin. Her teaching and research interests are in agribusiness management and entrepreneurship, groundwater management policies, climate change impacts on agriculture, and the scholarship of teaching and learning. Dr. Tewari is an active member of the Agricultural & Applied Economics Association, the Southern Agricultural Economics Association, and the Tennessee Academy of Sciences. For her teaching and scholarly work, Tewari was awarded the Cunningham Outstanding Teacher/Scholar Award (2020), The Ray and Wilma Smith Professorship (2018), the Hardy Graham Distinguished Professorship (2015), and was nominated for the University of Tennessee 2020 President’s Awards in the Discover Category. Dr. Tewari has worked with multiple education and outreach programs such as the UTM Mobile Energy Classroom and currently leads the CLASS (Climate Literacy for Agriculture and Sustainable Societies) initiative at UT Martin, funded by the United States Department of Agriculture's Capacity Building Grants.

Mark Simpson, Ph.D.

Co-Principal Investigator
Professor of Geography
College of Agricultural and Applied Sciences
201-D Johnson EPS
rsimpso5@utm.edu

Dr. Robert Mark Simpson is a Professor of Geography with the Department of Agriculture, Geosciences, and Natural Resources at the University of Tennessee at Martin. His teaching and research interests are in the fields of synoptic climatology, meteorology, geographic information systems and remote sensing. Since joining UTM in 1996, he has been instrumental in revising the curriculum offered by the department by helping to develop and initiate a concentration in meteorology, and a geographic information systems certification program. Dr. Simpson is currently participating in a USDA funded grant, “Building Capacity for Climate Change Education and Research in western Kentucky and west Tennessee.” This is a collaborative effort with Dr. Rachna Tewari and Dr. Joey Mehlhorn at UT-Martin, and Dr. Brian Parr and Dr. Naveen Musunuru of Murray State University. He participated in “Project RESET” a program offering online courses in earth systems science to middle school and high school teachers in Tennessee from 2002-2004 and has offered online courses in the new master’s degree in Education with an emphasis in Geoscience and with the Master’s Degree in Agriculture and Natural Resources. The program was part of the Earth Systems Science Alliance funded through NASA and the Institute for Global Environmental Strategies. He is currently active as a member of the American Meteorological Society, the American Association of Geographers, the Tennessee Academy of Science, and is an associate member of the American Association of State Climatologists.

Joey Mehlhorn, Ph.D.

Co-Principal Investigator
Parker Chair of Excellence
Professor of Agricultural Economics
College of Agricultural and Applied Sciences
254 Brehm Hall
jmehlhor@utm.edu

Dr. Mehlhorn serves as the Gilbert Parker Chair of Excellence in Agriculture and Natural Resources at the University of Tennessee at Martin. Dr. Mehlhorn’s responsibilities include directing the graduate program in agriculture and natural resources and teaching in the area of strategic and risk management. Prior to serving as the Parker Chair of Excellence he served as the chair of the Agriculture, Geosciences and Natural Resources department. In 2009, Mehlhorn was awarded the Hardy M. Graham Distinguished Professor for his work in teaching and scholarship. His educational background includes a Ph.D. in agricultural economics from Mississippi State University. Research interests include international agriculture, agriculture policy and entrepreneurship.

Bryanna Allison

Graduate Researcher

brymhugg@ut.utm.edu

Bryanna Allison was born in a small town just barely north of the Mississippi border, in Selmer, Tennessee. Though she was born in the rural community in McNairy County, she split most of her childhood years in South Carolina, Louisiana, Arizona, and West Virginia. She graduated from the University of Tennessee at Martin with her bachelor’s degree in December 2016 with a major in secondary education in earth and space. After teaching relatively few years in various middle schools, she went on to pursue her master’s degree in natural resource system management with a focus on climate research. She currently works as a sixth-grade teacher with the Tennessee Virtual Academy at Stride (formerly K12) and divides her time between her husband and three daughters.

Montana Wright

Undergraduate Researcher

moncwrig@ut.utm.edu

Ms. Montana Wright is a veterinarian health technician major at the University of Tennessee at Martin. Her research interests include the application and benefits of utilizing climate data in agricultural industries, specifically livestock production. In addition, she is the owner/operator of MCW farms, a multi-species livestock operation. Montana focuses on breeding commercial and registered Boer goats, and competing in American Boer Goat Association (ABGA) sanctioned shows across the nation. She is currently an undergraduate student researcher with the UT Martin-Murray State Climate Change Education Initiative funded by the USDA-Non-Land Grant Colleges of Agriculture Program. Her current research focuses on studying climatic parameters and livestock weights and prices in the midsouth region of the United States. Montana has recently presented her research findings at the 5th International Conference on Climate Change (ICCC), in February 2021. She also presented a poster on the application of climatic data and won 2nd place in the student competition at the 130th Tennessee Academy of Science Annual Meetings (TAS), in November 2020.

Anna Gafford

Undergraduate Researcher

annlgaff@ut.utm.edu

Anna Gafford is an undergraduate student of Agricultural Economics in the Department of Agriculture, Geosciences, and Natural Resources at the University of Tennessee at Martin. She is a junior agricultural business major with a minor in business administration. Ms. Gafford is a member of The National Society of Leadership and Success. She is currently working on a USDA research grant for Building Capacity for Climate Change Education (CCE) and Expanding Research Opportunities in Rural Communities in Tennessee and Kentucky. Ms. Gafford is currently conducting a research study on calculating carbon emissions from agricultural production in West Tennessee, specifically through no-till and conventional till cropping practices. She has recently presented her research findings for An Initial Study on Calculating Carbon Emissions from Agricultural Production in West Tennessee at the 5th Annual International Conference on Climate Change 2021.
